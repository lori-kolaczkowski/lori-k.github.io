I’m a surfer, artist, (marine) biologist, and now a statistician/data scientist – I’ve gotten into a lot of different things in my life. For all of my professional career, I’ve served biological/environmental research and management missions in federal government roles - 8 years with NOAA Fisheries using my marine biology degree to report and inspect data used in fishery management decisions, and now at the U.S. Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) using my master’s degree in statistics to perform a variety of data science tasks in support of toxicology research.

My role at the EPA is to ensure that the environmental scientists who are working to inform chemical regulations which protect human health and the environment through toxicology research have the data they need at their fingertips, and that the data is both rich in information and in quality. I work on 2 multidisciplinary teams (ToxCast/tcpl team and ToxRef team) to support the databases these scientists rely on heavily through data curation, pipelining new data to the databases, applying extensive quality assurance checks to both new data as it enters and to pre-existing data when updates are made, assisting in pipelining package development, identifying major bugs in the pipelining software package and collaborating on bug fixes, preparing analyses and summaries of the data for publicly released database documentation, and preparing publicly released vignette documentation for the pipelining package, tcplv3.0.

Learn more about ToxCast and tcpl here or about ToxRef here.

Although I’m gaining experience and skills crucial for a data science career in my current role, I desire a heavier focus on gaining insights from data through use of statistical methodologies, and thus, I am seeking a full-time opportunity which will offer challenges more commensurate with my statistical ability – one in which my work will involve more statistical modeling, whether explanatory or predictive, statistical tests, uncertainty quantification, etc. Though all methodologies learned in school were fascinating, I most enjoyed time series modeling, Bayesian modeling, and machine learning. I’m most interested in roles where I can spend a fair portion of my time doing this kind of work with a little room for creativity, and would happily do this in any industry.
